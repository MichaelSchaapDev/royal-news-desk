Good evening, and welcome to the Royal News Desk, where we separate
palace fact from palace fiction. Tonight we look at four stories that
set the internet alight this week.

# Palace Denies Secret Abdication Plan
A viral post claimed the King signed abdication papers last Tuesday.
The truth is rather less dramatic. The document in question was a
routine patronage letter, photographed at an odd angle.

[PAUSE]

Buckingham Palace issued a short statement calling the claim, quote,
entirely without foundation.

# The Vanishing Coronation Portrait
Several accounts reported that the official coronation portrait had
been quietly removed from the National Gallery. It has not. The
painting was moved two rooms over for a lighting refurbishment,
which the gallery announced back in March.

# Did the Princess Really Ban Photographers?
No. The so-called photographer ban was a standard privacy request
for the children's first day of school, identical to requests made
every year since 2013.

# And Finally: The Corgi That Broke the Internet
A photograph of a corgi wearing a tiny crown was shared forty
thousand times as, quote, the King's new companion. The dog belongs
to a bakery owner in Leeds. Her name is Biscuit, and she is, by all
accounts, a very good girl.

That is all for tonight. Join us next week for more royal
rumour checking. Good night.
